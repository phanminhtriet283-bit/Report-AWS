Here is the list and brief introduction of the AWS blog posts translated by the team:

### [Blog 1 – Journey from Statistics Enthusiast to NFL Analytics Pioneer: Mike Band](3.1-Blog1/)

This blog recounts the journey of **Mike Band**, who turned his childhood passion for sports statistics into a groundbreaking data analytics career at **NFL Next Gen Stats**. The article describes his development through education and practical experience, before contributing to building advanced AI models that helped change how the NFL analyzes and approaches game data. Alongside his technical achievements, Band also shares valuable advice for the next generation of engineers and analysts—stressing curiosity, a proactive spirit, and continuous learning as keys to long-term success.

### [Blog 2 – Agentic AI Assistant for SAP with AWS Generative AI](3.2-Blog2/)

This blog, Part 1 of a two-part series, details how to build a smart AI assistant (Agentic AI) using **Amazon Bedrock** to seamlessly interact with **SAP** systems. The article illustrates how to integrate **Generative AI** to allow users to query order data (from SAP) and logistics information (from DynamoDB) using natural language through common interfaces like **Microsoft Teams, Slack, and Streamlit**. This solution democratizes access to enterprise data without requiring deep technical knowledge of SQL or SAP structures.

### [Blog 3 – Amazon CloudFront Supports IPv6 Origins for End-to-End IPv6 Delivery](3.3-Blog3/)

This blog announces that **Amazon CloudFront** now supports **IPv6 connectivity from the edge to the origin**, enabling comprehensive end-to-end IPv6 delivery. The article explains crucial technical benefits such as eliminating NAT overhead, enhancing packet processing efficiency, and improving scalability for modern mobile networks. The authors also provide detailed instructions on how to configure a CloudFront distribution to enable the IPv6 or Dual-stack feature for the origin.
